Understanding Your Wove Score

Every product gets a simple A to F grade based on fabric composition, sustainability, and health impact.

A+

95-100% natural fibers

Excellent. Made entirely from natural, sustainable materials like organic cotton, linen, wool, or silk. No synthetics. Minimal to no chemical treatments.

B

70-94% natural fibers

Good. Mostly natural materials with small amounts of synthetics (like spandex for stretch). Low microplastic risk and chemical concerns.

D

30-69% natural fibers

Fair. Mixed blend with moderate to high synthetic content. Moderate microplastic risk and potential chemical concerns.

F

Below 30% natural fibers

Poor. Primarily synthetic materials like polyester, nylon, or acrylic. High microplastic risk and chemical concerns. Consider alternatives.

What We Analyze

  • Natural Fiber % — Cotton, linen, wool, silk, hemp
  • Synthetic Materials — Polyester, nylon, acrylic, rayon
  • Microplastic Risk — Shedding potential during washing
  • Chemical Treatments — PFAS, formaldehyde, flame retardants
  • Sustainability — Production impact, biodegradability

Our scoring algorithm is continuously updated based on the latest research about fabric safety, sustainability, and health impacts. We prioritize transparency over marketing claims.

Why Your Score Matters

The clothes you wear every day impact your health and the environment

🧬

Your Health

Synthetic fabrics can contain hormone-disrupting chemicals and shed microplastics that may be absorbed through your skin.

🌊

The Environment

Washing synthetic clothing releases millions of microplastic particles into waterways, harming marine life and ecosystems.

♻️

Sustainability

Natural fibers are biodegradable and renewable. Synthetics are petroleum-based and persist in landfills for hundreds of years.
